Abstract

This survey aims to analyze consumer preferences for different laptop brands and identify factors influencing their choices. The data reveals that HP is the most preferred brand, followed closely by Asus, Dell, and Lenovo, indicating their strong market presence and reputation for reliability, affordability, and performance.

HP’s dominance suggests that consumers value durability, diverse product offerings, and strong after-sales support. Asus is recognized for gaming and high-performance models, while Dell remains a popular choice for students and professionals. Lenovo’s appeal lies in its budget-friendly yet reliable options, making it a strong competitor in the market.

Brands like Acer and Apple have moderate user preference. Acer attracts budget-conscious buyers, while Apple’s MacBooks cater to users who prioritize premium design, security, and seamless ecosystem integration. However, Apple’s high price point may limit its accessibility to a broader audience.

Honor ranks the lowest in consumer preference, which may be due to low brand recognition, limited availability, or a lack of competitive features. To strengthen its position, Honor needs to focus on enhanced marketing, improved product features, and wider distribution channels.

The results highlight that brand reputation, performance, affordability, and features play a critical role in consumer decision-making. Established brands continue to dominate due to their strong customer trust, while lesser-known brands face challenges in gaining market traction.

Ultimately, this survey provides valuable insights into current market trends and consumer expectations, emphasizing the need for brands to continuously innovate, improve customer engagement, and offer competitive pricing to stay relevant in the evolving laptop industry.
